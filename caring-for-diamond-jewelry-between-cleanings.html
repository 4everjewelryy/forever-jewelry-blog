Caring for Your Diamond Jewelry Between Professional Cleanings

Simple at-home habits that keep your diamonds sparkling — and protect the settings that hold them.

Diamonds are famously tough, but their sparkle isn't self-cleaning. Everyday lotion, soap and skin oils build up behind the stone and quietly dull its brilliance. The good news: keeping diamond jewelry looking its best at home takes just a few minutes a week, and it's easy to do safely. Here's how — straight from the bench at Forever Jewelry.

The safe at-home cleaning method

  • Fill a bowl with warm water and a little mild dish soap.
  • Soak the piece for 20–30 minutes to loosen oils and grime.
  • Gently scrub with a soft toothbrush, paying attention to the back of the stone and any crevices where buildup hides.
  • Rinse thoroughly in warm water — plug the drain first so nothing slips away.
  • Pat dry with a clean, soft cloth.

Doing this about once a week keeps everyday brilliance from fading.

What to avoid

Skip harsh stuff

No bleach, toothpaste or abrasive cleaners — they can damage metal and finishes like rhodium plating.

Take it off sometimes

Remove diamonds for chores, workouts and swimming. Chlorine and harsh chemicals can harm both the stone's setting and the metal.

Smart storage

Diamonds can scratch other jewelry — and each other. Store pieces separately in soft pouches or a lined box with compartments, rather than tossing everything in one tray. A little organization prevents a lot of avoidable wear.

Don't skip the six-month checkup

Home care handles sparkle, but it can't tell you if a prong is wearing thin. About every six months, bring your pieces to a jeweler for a deep clean and inspection — we check that prongs are tight and stones are secure, catching a loose setting before you lose a stone.
