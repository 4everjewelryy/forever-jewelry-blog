Walk into any jewelry store in 2026 and you'll be asked a question that didn't exist a generation ago: lab-grown or natural? It's now the biggest decision most couples make when shopping for a diamond — lab-grown stones account for the majority of engagement-ring center stones today. As a Greenwood jeweler that sells both, our job isn't to push you one way. It's to explain the real differences honestly so you can spend your money where it matters most to you.

Are they the same thing?

Physically, yes. A lab-grown diamond is real diamond — pure crystallized carbon with the same optical, physical and chemical properties as a natural one. It sparkles the same, scratches the same, and passes a standard diamond tester. Under a jeweler's loupe you cannot tell them apart; only advanced lab equipment that reads the stone's growth pattern can. The single real difference is origin: a natural diamond formed in the earth over a very long time, while a lab-grown diamond is created in a controlled facility in a matter of weeks using one of two proven methods (HPHT or CVD).

Which should you choose?

Choose lab-grown if…

You want the biggest, brightest stone for your budget. The savings are dramatic — often 80–90% — which lets you go larger, choose a nicer setting, or simply spend less. Ideal if the diamond's look matters more than long-term resale.

Choose natural if…

The origin story and long-term value matter to you. Natural diamonds are naturally rare and tend to hold more of their value over time, which appeals to buyers who see the stone as an heirloom or investment.

There's no universally "right" answer — only the right answer for your priorities. Both are beautiful, both are real diamonds, and both come properly certified.

A note on certification

Whichever you choose, buy a certified stone. Reputable labs such as GIA and IGI grade both lab-grown and natural diamonds on the same 4Cs — cut, color, clarity and carat. Lab-grown reports clearly state the stone is laboratory-grown, and the diamond is laser-inscribed with its report number. Frequently asked questions

Are lab-grown diamonds real?

Yes — they're real diamonds with identical properties to natural ones. The only difference is that they're grown in a lab rather than formed in the earth.

How much cheaper are they?

Typically 80–93% less than a comparable natural diamond. A 1-carat lab-grown stone often runs about $700–$1,500 versus $3,500–$5,000 natural.

Do lab-grown diamonds hold value?

Generally less than natural — roughly 20–40% resale versus 50%+ for natural. Worth weighing if resale matters to you.

Are they certified?

Yes, by GIA and IGI on the same 4Cs, clearly marked as lab-grown and laser-inscribed.
