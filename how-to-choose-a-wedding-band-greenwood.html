Unlike the engagement ring, the wedding band is the piece you'll wear every single day — so comfort and durability matter as much as looks. Three decisions cover most of it: metal, width, and fit. Here's a quick, honest guide so you can choose with confidence at Forever Jewelry in Greenwood.

Choosing the metal

MetalBest for
14K goldA balance of durability, value and classic looks. Available in white, yellow and rose.
PlatinumThe most durable precious metal and hypoallergenic — a premium choice that lasts.
TungstenExtremely scratch-resistant and affordable, great for active hands — but can't be resized.
TitaniumVery light and durable at a low price — also generally not resizable.

One important note: tungsten and titanium can't be resized, so if your size may change, gold or platinum is the safer long-term choice.

Picking a width

Width is mostly about proportion and feel. Women commonly choose 2–3mm bands, while men often start around 6mm — substantial without being heavy. Slimmer hands often look best at 5mm; broader hands or anyone wanting a bolder statement may prefer 7–8mm. Wider bands make more of a statement but can feel heavier, so trying a few on is the best test.

Comfort fit vs. standard fit

Comfort fit

A rounded, domed interior that rests lightly on the finger and slides on easily — especially nice in wider bands. Runs about half a size larger than standard.

Standard fit

A flat interior that can feel a bit snugger, particularly above 6mm. Some people prefer the secure feel.

Getting your size right is the foundation of comfort — visit us for an accurate measurement, and remember fingers swell slightly in heat or after activity.

Setting your budget

Wedding bands span a wide range, and there's a great option at almost any budget. Tungsten and titanium keep costs low; gold and platinum cost more but can be resized and refinished for life. Frequently asked questions

Best metal?

14K gold balances value and durability; platinum is most durable; tungsten/titanium are scratch-resistant and affordable but not resizable.

What width?

Women often 2–3mm, men often ~6mm. Try widths on to find your proportion.

What's comfort fit?

A domed interior that slides on easily and feels great in wider bands — runs about half a size larger.

Can tungsten/titanium be resized?

Generally no — get the size right up front, or choose gold/platinum if your size may change.
