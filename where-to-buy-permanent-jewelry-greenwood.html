What is permanent jewelry?

Permanent jewelry is a delicate chain — a bracelet, anklet or necklace — that is measured to your exact size and joined with a tiny weld instead of a traditional clasp. Because there's no clasp to unhook, the piece sits seamlessly on your wrist, ankle or neck and stays on through showers, workouts, swimming and sleep. People choose it to mark a friendship, a relationship or a milestone, or simply because they love an everyday piece they never have to fuss with.

The "permanent" part sounds intimidating, but it isn't. The chain can be removed at any time with small scissors at the weld point, and a jeweler can re-weld it for you afterward. It's a commitment to convenience, not something irreversible.

How welded permanent jewelry works

The process is quick and painless. A jeweler custom-fits the chain to your body so it's comfortable and won't pinch or slide, then uses a small pulse-arc welder to fuse the two ends together at a single point. The welding arc never touches your skin, so you feel nothing at all — most people are surprised by how fast it is. From choosing your chain to walking out wearing it, a single piece usually takes about ten to fifteen minutes.

There are no needles, no piercing and no recovery time. You walk out wearing a finished piece the same day.

Which metal should you choose?

The metal you pick determines how your piece wears over the years, so it's worth a quick conversation with your jeweler:

Solid 14K gold

The most durable choice for everyday wear. Solid 14K gold resists tarnish and discoloration and holds up beautifully over time, which makes it the go-to for a piece you truly never take off. It costs more than silver, but it's an investment in longevity.

Sterling silver

A more affordable precious metal and a popular starting point. Silver can oxidize faster with heavy exposure to chlorine or salt water, so it benefits from occasional care, but it's a beautiful, budget-friendly option.

A trustworthy jeweler will tell you honestly which chain suits your lifestyle and budget rather than steering you toward the most expensive option. If you swim daily or work with your hands, that conversation matters.
