Start with the 4Cs

Every diamond is graded on four characteristics:

  • Cut — how well the stone is shaped and faceted. This has the biggest impact on sparkle, so don't skimp here.
  • Color — how colorless the diamond is. Near-colorless grades often look white to the eye for far less money.
  • Clarity — internal characteristics. Many "eye-clean" stones have small inclusions you simply can't see without magnification.
  • Carat — the weight. Bigger isn't always better; a well-cut smaller stone can outshine a larger dull one.

The trick is balancing the four to your taste and budget rather than maxing out one. We'll show you stones side by side so you can see the difference your money makes.

Setting and metal

The setting

Solitaire, halo, three-stone, pavé — the setting shapes the whole look and affects how large the center stone appears. A halo, for example, makes a center diamond look bigger.

The metal

White gold, yellow gold, rose gold and platinum each have a different look and price. Platinum is the most durable; 14K gold is a popular, hard-wearing, affordable choice.
